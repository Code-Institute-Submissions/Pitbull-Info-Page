The American Pitbull Terrier: A Misunderstood Breed

This website is dedicated to educating the public about the American Pitbull Terrier (APBT) and explaining the misconceptions assosciated with this loyal family dog. This website also includes a gallery page and a quiz page, where you can test your newfound knowledge about the pitbull terrier.

Breed Characteristics

  • Size: Medium sized dog breed
  • Height (Adult): Male - 45-53cm Female - 43-50cm
  • Weight (Adult): Male - 16–27 kg, Female - 14–23 kg
  • Colors: Black, White, Brindle, Fawn, Tan, Grey, Brown, Blue, Red
  • Temperament: Loyal, Loving, Protective, Intelligent, Stubborn, Brave, Attached, Aloof at times
  • Lifespan: 8 - 15 years

Breed Misconceptions

Stereotypes:

  • Pitbulls are dangerous, vicious dogs bred only to fight
  • Pitbulls do not get along with children or other animals
  • Pitbulls have locking jaws
  • Pitbulls are more likely to bite and attack humans than other dogs
  • American bullies are Pitbulls

In Reality:

  • Pitbulls are loving family dogs bred initially to hunt small game such as rodents
  • Pitbulls get along perfectly with other animals and children, having been socialised from an early age
  • Pitbull's jaws do not lock, but their resillience means that when they bite they tend to hold on. This is great for protection work
  • Pitbulls are not more likely to bite humans than other dog breeds. In fact, they have been bred to be extremely socialised with humans as compared with breeds like the Cane Corso
  • American bullys are not Pitbulls. The American Pitbull Terrier is it's own breed, as is the American bully. However, both do look somewhat similar due to the APBT being an ancestor breed to the American Bully.
